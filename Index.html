What is tillamook rock lighthouse?

tillamook lighthouse is a now abandoned 1800s era lighthouse off the coast of oregon. the lighthouse was constructed to help sailors navigate the dangerouse tillamook head, sailors gave the lighthouse the name "the terrible tilly" due to the danger of the waters surrounding it. the lighthouse was constructed on an isolated basalt rock where keepers worked in 42 day rotations followed by 21 days off. this work period is shorter ten the usual shift of 3 monthes, due to the mental and physical effects of working in such an isolated and volatile enviroment, the keepers had to work much short shifts.

The lighthouse has been closed since 1957 and is now a part of the Oregon National Wildlife Refuge. the property remains a staple of the past in technology and in ledgend, with much local ledgend surrounding it. if your in the area, tillamook lighthouse is a must see.
